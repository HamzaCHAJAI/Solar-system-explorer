Exploring the Red Planet

Mars, nicknamed the Red Planet, owes its color to iron oxide covering its surface. The fourth planet in the Solar System, it shares many similarities with Earth: a comparable day length, seasons, and polar ice caps, making it a prime target for space exploration. Its surface features impressive landmarks such as Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the Solar System, and Valles Marineris, a vast canyon system. Its very thin atmosphere, composed mainly of carbon dioxide, leads to extreme temperatures and violent dust storms. Scientists have discovered many traces of ancient water, suggesting that Mars may have been habitable in the past. Today, it lies at the heart of future mission plans and represents a key step toward a possible human presence beyond Earth.
