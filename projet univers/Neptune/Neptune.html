The blue planet

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet in the Solar System, an ice giant known for its deep blue color caused by methane in its atmosphere. Slightly smaller than Uranus but more massive, it experiences some of the fastest winds in the Solar System, reaching over 2,000 km/h. Its atmosphere features massive storms, such as the Great Dark Spot, and shifting cloud bands. Neptune has a diverse moon system, including Triton, which shows cryovolcanism and orbits in a retrograde direction, indicating it was likely captured. Being very far from the Sun, Neptune is extremely cold and dim, yet it remains a fascinating natural laboratory to study ice giants and extreme atmospheric dynamics in the universe.
