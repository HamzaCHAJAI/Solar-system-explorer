The Tilted Planet

Uranus is an ice giant recognizable by its blue-green color, caused by methane in its atmosphere. The seventh planet in the Solar System, it stands out for an extreme feature: an axial tilt of about 98°, meaning it almost “rolls” along its orbit. This tilt causes long and extreme seasons, each lasting more than 20 years. Its atmosphere is extremely cold, with some of the fastest winds in the Solar System and temperatures dropping below −220 °C, making Uranus one of the coldest known planets. Uranus also has faint rings and around thirty moons. Long overlooked, it is now considered essential for understanding the formation of ice giants and similar exoplanets, which are very common in the universe.
