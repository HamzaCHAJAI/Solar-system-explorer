Journey to the Gas Giant

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, a gas giant whose mass is more than 300 times that of Earth. It is mainly composed of hydrogen and helium, has no solid surface, and features a turbulent atmosphere marked by colorful bands and the famous Great Red Spot, a gigantic anticyclone active for centuries. Jupiter has an impressive system of moons — more than 95 confirmed — among which Europa, Ganymede, and Io display unique characteristics such as subsurface oceans or intense volcanic activity. Its powerful gravity strongly influences the rest of the Solar System, indirectly protecting Earth from many asteroids and comets. With its faint rings and enormous magnetic field, Jupiter remains a fascinating planet, both colossal and mysterious.
